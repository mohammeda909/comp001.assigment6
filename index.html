List Of Inventors Killed By Their Own Inventions

Automotive

Inventors in the automotive field often risked their lives testing high-speed vehicles or innovative safety features. For instance, Franz Reichelt tragically died testing his wearable parachute suit by jumping off the Eiffel Tower in 1912.

Maritime

Maritime inventors faced perils such as experimental submarine malfunctions. Horace Lawson Hunley, the Confederate inventor of the first combat submarine, drowned alongside his crew during a test in 1863.

Medical

Medical pioneers sometimes tested their creations on themselves. Marie Curie succumbed to radiation exposure after years of groundbreaking research into radioactive materials, laying the foundation for modern medicine.

Physics

Physicists have pursued their theories despite dangerous conditions. Nikola Tesla, though not directly killed by his inventions, experienced significant harm from the high-voltage experiments that defined his career.

Popular Myths

Some stories about inventors dying from their inventions are apocryphal or exaggerated. For example, the tale of a Segway inventor dying while riding a Segway has been debunked as a case of mistaken identity.
